# Communications Plan

How information moves on the Aurora launch — who hears what, how often, and through which channel. The rule of thumb: decisions are written down, status is pushed (not pulled), and bad news travels fast.

<!-- otion:info {"color":"blue","icon":"Info","text":"**Default channel** is the `#aurora-launch` team space. Anything that changes scope, date, or budget also goes in a written status and is raised at the next steering committee."} -->

## Cadence

- **Daily** — Engineering and Operations standup, 09:15, 15 minutes. Blockers only.
- **Weekly** — Friday status report (see the linked page). Pushed to the sponsor and steering committee.
- **Biweekly** — Steering committee review. Gate decisions, risk review, budget check-in.
- **Per gate** — Go / no-go review with the Accountable stakeholder before each phase transition.

## Channels by audience

- **Executive sponsor** — one-page Friday status, plus an immediate heads-up on any gate slip.
- **Steering committee** — biweekly review deck and the live risk register.
- **Core team** — `#aurora-launch` space, daily standups, and the task board.
- **Wider company** — monthly all-hands slide and a launch-week internal announcement.

## Escalation path

When something is blocked for more than 48 hours, escalate in this order:

1. Raise it with the workstream lead.
2. If unresolved, bring it to the Program Lead (Priya Raman).
3. If it threatens the launch date, escalate to the executive sponsor (Marcus Feld) the same day.
