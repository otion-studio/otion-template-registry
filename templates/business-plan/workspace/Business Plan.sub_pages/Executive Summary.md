# Executive Summary

NestRoute is a subscription app that turns the weekly question "what's for dinner?" into a thirty-second answer. Households set their size, dietary needs, and budget once; each week NestRoute proposes a dinner plan, consolidates it into one grocery list, and lays out a short Sunday prep routine. We charge a flat 12 USD a month with no upsells.

## The problem

Cooking at home is cheaper and healthier than the alternatives, but the planning tax is real: deciding meals, building a list that doesn't double-buy, and prepping on a weeknight. Most people solve this with a patchwork of notes apps, screenshots, and good intentions — and fall back to takeout when the week gets busy.

## The solution

One app that owns the whole loop: plan, shop, prep. NestRoute already knows your household and your pantry staples, so each week's plan needs a tap to accept and an edit or two to personalize, not an hour of setup.

## Why now

The habit of paying a small monthly fee for a single, well-scoped tool is now normal for consumers. NestRoute fits the same slot as a music or storage subscription: cheap, sticky, and easy to justify because it saves time every single week.
