# Product

NestRoute is built around one weekly ritual and three jobs inside it: **plan**, **shop**, and **prep**. Everything in the app exists to make that Sunday loop take minutes instead of an hour.

## The weekly loop

1. **Plan.** NestRoute proposes seven dinners based on household size, dietary preferences, time budget, and what you cooked recently so meals don't repeat too soon. Swap any night with a tap.
2. **Shop.** The accepted plan collapses into one deduplicated grocery list, grouped by aisle, with pantry staples you already have filtered out.
3. **Prep.** A short Sunday routine batches the steps that pay off — chopping, marinating, par-cooking — so weeknights are assembly, not cooking from zero.

## Feature pillars

- **Household profiles** — sizes, allergies, dislikes, and a weekly time budget.
- **Pantry awareness** — mark staples once; the list stops re-buying them.
- **One-list shopping** — aisle-grouped, deduplicated, sized to the household.
- **Prep routines** — a realistic Sunday plan, not a wall of recipe cards.
- **Flexible swaps** — never feel boxed in by the suggested plan.

## What we are deliberately not building (yet)

- Grocery delivery of our own — we plan around the stores people already use, and pursue delivery as a *partnership* (see Go-to-Market).
- A social recipe network — the product is a private utility, not a feed.
- A free ad-supported tier — the value is the time saved, and we charge for it directly.

## Product principles

- **Respect the weeknight.** Every feature is judged by whether it removes friction from a busy evening.
- **Fewer taps, better defaults.** A good default plan beats a configurable empty one.
- **Earn the subscription every week.** If a household doesn't feel the time saved, they should be able to leave in two taps — and that honesty is what keeps churn at 3%.
