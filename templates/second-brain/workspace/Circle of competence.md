# Circle of competence

You don't need to know everything — you need to know the edges of what you know. The circle of competence is the domain where your judgment is actually reliable. Inside it, trust yourself. Outside it, slow down, ask for help, or pass.

Warren Buffett's framing is blunt: "The size of that circle is not very important; knowing its boundaries, however, is vital." Trouble rarely comes from operating within your competence. It comes from wandering outside it without noticing — confidently making decisions in a domain where your intuitions are miscalibrated.

The hard part is honesty about the boundary, because the same overconfidence that pulls you outside the circle also hides the edge from view. Useful tests: Can you explain it simply, [The Feynman technique](The Feynman technique.md)-style? Can you argue the other side? Would an expert recognise your reasoning? When in doubt, assume you're nearer the edge than you feel — and remember that the [Map is not the territory](Map is not the territory.md).
