# Compounding

Compounding is what happens when the output of a process feeds back into its input. Money earns interest, and that interest earns interest. Knowledge connects to knowledge, making the next idea easier to grasp. The effect is invisible early and overwhelming late, which is why almost everyone underestimates it.

The counterintuitive part is the shape of the curve. For a long time, compounding looks like nothing is happening — small, boring, linear gains. Then the curve bends sharply upward. Most people quit during the flat part because the payoff hasn't arrived yet. The discipline of compounding is mostly the discipline of not stopping.

This is why a second brain works. Each [Atomic notes](Atomic notes.md) entry is small, but linked into the web it raises the value of every other note — the collection compounds. The same logic drives [Spaced repetition](Spaced repetition.md) (memory built on memory) and habit formation (a small action, repeated, becomes identity).
