# The two-minute rule

If a task takes less than two minutes, do it now. David Allen built this into *Getting Things Done* for a simple reason: tracking a tiny task — writing it down, remembering it, returning to it — costs more than just doing it. The two-minute rule clears that clutter before it accumulates.

There's a second, sneakier version for building habits, from James Clear: shrink any new habit down to a two-minute version. "Read before bed" becomes "read one page." "Go for a run" becomes "put on running shoes." The point isn't the two minutes of work — it's defeating the inertia of starting. Once you've begun, continuing is easy; the hard part was the friction at the door.

Both versions attack the same enemy: the gap between intention and action. Small tasks pile into background anxiety; small starts never happen because starting feels big. The rule is a way to [Default to action](Default to action.md) on the smallest possible scale.
