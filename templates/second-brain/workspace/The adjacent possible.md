# The adjacent possible

At any moment, only certain next steps are reachable — the ideas, inventions, or moves that the current state makes possible. Stuart Kauffman called this the "adjacent possible," and Steven Johnson borrowed it to explain innovation: you can't jump straight to a distant breakthrough, but each new combination opens a fresh set of doors that weren't there before.

It explains why so many inventions arrive only when their prerequisites exist, and why the same idea is often discovered simultaneously by several people — the adjacent possible had just opened for everyone at once. You can't invent the search engine before the web, or the web before the network. Progress moves one door at a time, and every door you walk through reveals new ones.

For thinking, the lesson is to maximise your adjacent possible: the more varied material you hold and connect, the more new combinations become reachable from where you stand. This is exactly what a dense note network does — every link you add expands the set of next ideas, which is why [Idea emergence](Idea emergence.md) and [Atomic notes](Atomic notes.md) are the practical machinery behind it.
