# Decision-making

Most of life's outcomes are downstream of a few important decisions made under uncertainty. You rarely have full information, so the goal isn't to be right every time — it's to have a process that's right on average and that limits the damage when you're wrong.

A good process leans on [Mental Models](Mental Models.md): invert the problem, trace the [Second-order thinking](Second-order thinking.md) consequences, and weigh the [Opportunity cost](Opportunity cost.md) of every "yes." It also means knowing where you actually have an edge — your [Circle of competence](Circle of competence.md) — and preferring choices that get *stronger* under stress, the logic of [Antifragility](Antifragility.md).
