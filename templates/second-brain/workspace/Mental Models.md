# Mental Models

A mental model is a compressed way of seeing — a thinking tool you can carry from one situation to another. No single model is true everywhere; the skill is keeping a latticework of them and reaching for the one that fits. Charlie Munger called this "a latticework of models," and it's the backbone of clear [Decision-making](Decision-making.md).

These models earn their keep by being reusable. [Inversion](Inversion.md) flips a problem around; [Second-order thinking](Second-order thinking.md) asks what happens next; [First principles](First principles.md) rebuilds an idea from the ground up. Use them together.
