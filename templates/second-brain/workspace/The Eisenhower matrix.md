# The Eisenhower matrix

"What is important is seldom urgent, and what is urgent is seldom important." The line is attributed to Dwight Eisenhower, and it became a simple two-by-two grid: sort every task by *urgent vs. not urgent* and *important vs. not important*. The four quadrants tell you what to do with each.

**Important and urgent** — do it now (real crises, hard deadlines). **Important but not urgent** — schedule it (the deep, strategic work that actually shapes your life). **Urgent but not important** — delegate or minimise it (most interruptions and other people's emergencies). **Neither** — delete it. The trap most people fall into is living entirely in the urgent column, reacting all day while the important-but-not-urgent work — health, relationships, [Deep work](Deep work.md) — silently never gets done.

The matrix is really a tool for seeing [Opportunity cost](Opportunity cost.md): every hour spent in the "urgent but unimportant" quadrant is an hour stolen from the "important" one. Use it to *schedule* importance before urgency floods in and decides for you.
