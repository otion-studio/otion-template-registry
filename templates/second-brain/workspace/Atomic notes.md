# Atomic notes

An atomic note holds exactly one idea. Not a topic, not a chapter, not everything you know about productivity — one self-contained thought, small enough to grasp at a glance and to link from anywhere. The atomicity is the whole point: a note about one idea can connect to a note about another, and those connections are where understanding compounds.

Why one idea per note? Because a note packed with five ideas can only be filed in one place and linked as one lump. Split those five into five atomic notes and each can join a different web, be referenced precisely, and recombine with notes you haven't even written yet. Granularity is what makes the network dense — and density is what makes it generative.

The discipline is small but strict: when a note starts covering two things, split it. Give each note a title that states its claim ("Constraints breed creativity," not "Notes on creativity"), so the title alone tells you what's inside and makes linking effortless. Atomic notes are the bricks; [Evergreen notes](Evergreen notes.md) describes how to keep them alive, and the value they create over time is pure [Compounding](Compounding.md).
