# Learning

Learning is not the same as exposure. You can re-read a chapter ten times and remember almost nothing; you can struggle to recall it once and remember it for years. The notes in this cluster are about the difference — about practising *retrieval* and *difficulty* rather than the comfortable illusion of familiarity.

The core moves: space your reviews so forgetting works for you ([Spaced repetition](Spaced repetition.md)), practise at the edge of your ability with feedback ([Deliberate practice](Deliberate practice.md)), and expose the gaps in your understanding by explaining things simply ([The Feynman technique](The Feynman technique.md)). Hold it all with a [Beginner's mind](Beginner's mind.md), because expertise quietly narrows what you're willing to see.
