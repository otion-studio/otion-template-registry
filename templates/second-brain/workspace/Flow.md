# Flow

Flow is the state of complete absorption in an activity — so engaged that time, self-consciousness, and effort seem to disappear. The psychologist Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi spent decades studying it and found it's where people report their most satisfying and productive work, across wildly different domains from chess to rock climbing to surgery.

Flow has reliable triggers. The most important is the *challenge–skill balance*: the task has to be hard enough to demand your full attention but not so hard that it tips into anxiety. Too easy and you drift into boredom; too hard and you panic. The sweet spot is the narrow channel between them. Flow also needs clear goals and immediate feedback, so you always know whether the last move worked.

This is why flow and [Deep work](Deep work.md) are siblings — protected, undistracted focus is the doorway to flow. It's also why [Deliberate practice](Deliberate practice.md) tends to live just outside flow: practice deliberately pushes into the uncomfortable zone where you're failing, while flow lives just below it, where you're stretched but succeeding.
