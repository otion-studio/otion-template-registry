# World War I

The first industrial-scale total war, 1914–1918. The same machinery that built the modern economy now powered the killing — a direct line from the last unit to this one.

## Causes: the MAIN framework

- **M**ilitarism — arms races, especially the Anglo-German naval rivalry.
- **A**lliances — two rigid blocs: Triple Alliance vs. Triple Entente.
- **I**mperialism — competition for colonies and markets.
- **N**ationalism — ethnic tensions, especially in the Balkans.

The **spark**: the assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand in Sarajevo, 28 June 1914, which the alliance system turned into a continental war within weeks.

## Timeline

- **1914 (Jun)** — Franz Ferdinand assassinated.
- **1914 (Aug)** — Germany invades Belgium; Britain declares war.
- **1914 (Sep)** — First Battle of the Marne halts the German advance; the front stalls.
- **1916** — Verdun and the Somme: months of attrition for almost no ground.
- **1917** — The U.S. enters the war; Russia exits after its revolution.
- **1918 (Nov 11)** — Armistice signed; fighting ends.

## Trench warfare and the Western Front

- A continuous line of trenches ran from the North Sea to Switzerland.
- **No man's land** between the lines was swept by machine guns and artillery.
- New weapons — poison gas, tanks, aircraft — could not break the deadlock cheaply.
- Result: enormous casualties for tiny territorial gains.

## Key terms

- **Total war** — the whole economy and society mobilized for the war effort.
- **Attrition** — wearing the enemy down by sustained losses rather than decisive maneuver.
- **Armistice** — the agreement to stop fighting (11 November 1918).
- **Treaty of Versailles (1919)** — the peace settlement that placed blame and reparations on Germany.
